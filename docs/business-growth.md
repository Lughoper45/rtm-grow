1   THE OPPORTUNITY

The Canadian Digital Adoption Program (CDAP) was the federal government's $4 billion initiative to help Canadian SMEs adopt digital technology. It supported over 71,000 businesses, disbursed $1.2 billion, and then closed in early 2024 — fully subscribed.

That closure left a massive, unmet gap. There are 1.2 million SMEs in Canada. CDAP reached fewer than 6% of them. The remaining 94% still need exactly what CDAP provided: digital strategy, website development, marketing, e-commerce, automation, and sales infrastructure — but no government program is currently funding it.

At the same time, Canada's immigrant and multicultural business community — the exact community RTM was built to serve — is the most underserved segment in this space. These are business owners who face language barriers, limited professional networks, and a digital services market that doesn't speak to their reality. RTM already has their trust. That is the starting advantage no competitor can buy.

1.2  The Post-CDAP Landscape
CDAP closed but the need didn't. In its place, a fragmented cottage industry emerged — hundreds of small agencies offering $15,000 digital adoption plans with questionable quality. Wikipedia literally notes that CDAP 'led to a cottage industry of questionably credentialed advisers.'

RTM can do what those advisors couldn't: deliver real, accountable, technology-backed digital growth services through a trusted platform that already has the business community's confidence. The government noticed that problem. RTM can be the solution when they fund it again.

Critical Insight
CDAP was a digital advisor registration program. RTM can register as an approved digital advisor for any successor program the moment ISED reopens applications.
The path to government recognition is not a long-term aspiration. RTM already meets the criteria that CDAP required: Canadian business, technology platform, SME advisory capability, multicultural reach.
RTM should build the Grow My Business division now — and be ready to formally register when the next federal digital adoption program opens.

5   THE PATH TO GOVERNMENT RECOGNITION

You asked specifically: how do we get recognized by the Canadian government for helping business? This section lays out the exact pathway — not as a long-term dream, but as a structured 18-month plan.

The Canadian government does not formally 'recognize' private businesses as approved helpers. What it does is register approved service providers within specific programs. RTM needs to pursue three parallel tracks:

5.1  Track 1 — Register as a CDAP Successor Digital Advisor
CDAP closed in February 2024 but supported 71,000 businesses. The federal government's Connecting Canadians agenda and the Budget 2025 AI and digital adoption commitments make a successor program highly likely in 2026–2027. RTM must be ready.

Requirement CDAP Had	RTM's Current Status
Canadian incorporation	RTM Global Canada — Canadian corporation, North York ON. PASS.
Technology platform for SME advisory	rtmbusinessdirectory.com — active Vite/React platform, 217 grants, Supabase, Stripe. PASS.
Digital advisory capability	Grant advisory exists. Growth Services division being built now. Needs to be documented as a service offering.
Proven SME outcomes	First members onboarded (Annabel Brown / Eugenecare). Case studies needed. Build starting now.
Multicultural / underserved reach	RTM's core identity. Immigrant business community focus is documented in platform content. STRONG ADVANTAGE.
No conflict of interest / vendor neutral advice	RTM advises on tools but does not exclusively sell one vendor. Must document vendor-neutral policy.

Action: Prepare RTM's CDAP-ready service documentation now. When the next program opens, RTM submits within the first week of registration.

5.2  Track 2 — Become a BIA (Business Improvement Area) Technology Partner
Toronto, Ottawa, Calgary, and Vancouver all have formal BIA networks representing thousands of local businesses. BIAs are actively looking for technology partners to help their member businesses grow digitally. Partnership with 3–5 major BIAs gives RTM:

•	Government-adjacent credibility — BIAs are funded by municipalities
•	Immediate access to hundreds of member businesses per BIA
•	Co-branding opportunities: 'RTM-powered digital growth, in partnership with [BIA name]'
•	Path to municipal grant funding — cities fund BIA technology programs
•	Case studies and testimonials from real, named businesses

Target BIAs: Scarborough BIA (largest concentration of RTM's existing community), Little Jamaica BIA, Chinatown BIA, Little Italy BIA, Mississauga city BIAs.

5.3  Track 3 — WES (Women Entrepreneurship Strategy) Ecosystem Partner
The federal WES Ecosystem Fund funds organizations that support women entrepreneurs — accelerators, incubators, mentorship programs. RTM's immigrant business community has a significant women-entrepreneur segment. Becoming a WES ecosystem partner means:

•	Up to $2M in federal funding to build out the growth services division
•	Recognition as a WES-approved platform — listed on ISED's women entrepreneur resources
•	Access to the national WES network of 30+ ecosystem organizations
•	Requirement: 51%+ of clients served must be women entrepreneurs OR RTM's programs must specifically benefit women

WES Eligibility Assessment
RTM's current client base (Annabel Brown / Eugenecare — women-owned home care) is exactly the WES target.
A dedicated 'Women in Business' stream within Grow My Business — with discounted packages for women-owned businesses — would meet the WES mandate.
Timeline: Apply to WES Ecosystem Fund Round 3 (expected late 2026 intake). Budget for application: 3 months of documentation work.

5.4  Track 4 — Digital Main Street Partnership
Digital Main Street is the City of Toronto program (now operating nationally) that helps local businesses get online. It pre-dated CDAP and outlasted it. RTM can apply to be a Digital Main Street registered service provider, giving access to:

•	City of Toronto endorsement and referrals
•	Listing on the Digital Main Street service provider marketplace
•	Co-marketing with Toronto Economic Development
•	Access to small business owners actively seeking digital help

Apply at: digitalmainstreet.ca/service-providers. Requirements: Canadian business, digital advisory services, demonstrated SME capability.

#	Question	Why It Matters
